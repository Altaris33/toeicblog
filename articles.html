TRANSHUMANISM

Today I would like to discuss about an interesting, even fascinating subject. This blog aims to talk to you every week about our discoveries combining scientific, technological and medical advances. So we have to ask ourselves why medicine and new technologies have been such a good combination in recent years. The quest for youth, the end of disease is a frantic race to push back our own limits, those of the human body.
The real purpose behind all this is trans-humanism. This movement of thought, a continuity of humanism, which aims to bring humanity to a level 2.0, superhuman or post-human. So that's already the case, just take a look at everything we modify, repair to be better: surgery for vision, surgery such as the fitting of prostheses. In short, we are already in this movement but here we are talking about overcoming the very idea of death and achieving immortality by merging man and computer. In its modern form, this trend has existed since the 1960s in California, but the boom is now considerable thanks to artificial intelligence and large companies like Google are not fooled and clearly state their objectives in this area/in this way. It is so exciting that one article is not enough to deal with the subject and all its facets, but I hope it arouses your curiosity and launches you into your own research.

REMOTE MEDICAL EXAMINATIONS

We all knew those days when we were so sick that even going to the doctor was unthinkable. A home doctor? Too expensive... The telehealth company TytoCare has developed a technology for remote medical examinations. The trials showed results as good as the in-person examinations. Indeed, a complete tool has been developed to take the temperature, listen to the heart, look at the ears, the throat... Doing a medical examination has never been easier! All data taken earlier is transferred to your doctor in real time, so he can give you a diagnosis or tell you if it is necessary to travel. This tool could be useful for people in rural areas or also for single parents or with a newborn child, who prefer the comfort of being at home to being on the road or waiting hours in emergencies. But perhaps others will be less enthusiastic and will see only one other way to collect data.

This time, I came across a video featuring Mary Lou Jepsen, an inventor in the field of imaging and computer equipment but not only. The idea is that light can be used to observe the depths of the body and brain. Okay, it's easy, it's the title! NANOTECHNOLOGY AND MEDECINE

You remember the article on light to detect tumours. If you haven't read it, check out the video that matches! After we detect the cancer, we'll see how to treat it. Even if cancer is no longer a fatality, one in two cancers is cured, it stills the leading cause of death in France. The so-called inoperable metastasis tumours could find their solutions in targeted physical approaches: nanoparticles. They can detect the tumour and accumulate there, deliver a drug with active ingredients, vibrate them to activate the mechanisms of cell death or increase the effectiveness of radiotherapy by absorbing X-rays and diffusing them locally. These nanoparticles really have everything they need to succeed, what do you think?

ESEARCHERS PRINT HUMAN SKIN DIRECTLY ON WOUNDS IN BORDEAUX

Poietis reproduces human skin for the cosmetics and pharmaceutical industry and, in the long term, for hospitals. Its patented 3D laser bio-printing technology has received one of Inpi's innovation trophies. Printing human skin is Poietis' challenge, which reproduces human biological tissues with its unique laser bio-printing process. It allows it to print tissue components with microscopic resolution and precision from living skin cells. The 3D printing of biological fabrics is done by layer by layer deposition of microdrops of biological "inks" (containing one to several cells). The technology reproduces human skin cell by cell and comes closer to human tissues. At the end of the bio-impression process, a cluster of living cells that continue to interact with each other to create the finished product by maturation. The start-up is based in Pessac (Gironde). The start-up, headed by Fabien Guillemot and Bruno Brisson, currently employs 22 people and generated annual sales of €361,000 in 2016. To finance itself, the SME used two successive fundraising campaigns carried out on the Wiseed participative platform. It will also benefit from cash advances as it is one of the winners of the 2017 World Innovation Competition, supported in particular by bpifrance. In total, the SME estimates that it can provide a total of €6 million in financing.
